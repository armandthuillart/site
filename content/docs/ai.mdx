## Basics of Prompting

You can achieve a lot with simple prompts, but the quality of results depends on how much information you provide it and how well-crafted the prompt is. 

Here are some tips to improve your prompts:

- **Be specific and clear**: Just like giving instructions to a human, prompts should clearly articulate the desired outcome. Ambiguity can lead to unexpected or irrelevant outputs.
- **Make it structured**: Structuring prompts logically by breaking them down into separate parts or steps can significantly enhance an LLM’s ability to understand and process information. Similarly, specifying the desired output format (e.g., a list, paragraph, or code snippet) improves response relevance.
- **Add delimiters**: Utilizing special characters as delimiters within prompts can further clarify the structure and segregate different elements, improving the model’s understanding.

[^1]: Artificial intelligence (AI) is the umbrella concept of machines and software that can perform tasks typically requiring human intelligence.

[^2]: A large language model (LLM) is the “mind” behind [ChatGPT](https://chatgpt.com/), [Claude](https://www.claude.com/), or [Gemini](https://gemini.google.com). It’s “large” because it learned from massive amounts of data, “language” because it reads and writes like humans, and “model” because there’s a lot of math involved.

[^3]: Prompting is the skill of writing questions in a way that maximizes the quality of the answers you get from an AI model. It can be using triple backticks to mark data blocks, or labels like `CONTEXT: ...`, `QUESTION: ...`, and `INSTRUCTIONS: ...`.
